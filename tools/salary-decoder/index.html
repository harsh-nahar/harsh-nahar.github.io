Why doesn't the math match?

If you are new to the Indian corporate world, the "CTC" figure is often the most confusing part. It stands for Cost to Company. Think of it as the total bill the company pays to keep you employed, not the check they hand you.

The biggest invisible chunk is usually the Employer's Contribution to PF. By law, 12% of your basic salary goes to your pension fund from your side. But the company also has to match that 12%. Instead of paying that extra 12% from their own pocket, most companies simply include it inside your CTC. So, technically, you are paying your own "employer share."

Then there is Gratuity. This is a loyalty bonus you only get if you stay for 5 years. If it's part of your CTC and you leave after 2 years, you basically donated that money back to the company.
